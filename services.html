STRESS AND ANXIETY

College students often say stress represents the biggest threat to their academic performance. Stress is different for every student. There are many ways to cope with stress. Some are simple and involve minor adjustments in your lifestyle. Other strategies require practice and effort. Feelings of overbearing anxiety and helplessness are manageable, but when they get paralyzing there is a need for intervention and strategic problem-solving.

DEPRESSION AND GRIEF

Grief results in a wave of overwhelming emotions and coping can be slow and painful. People process grief in different ways. Some cry and talk, others hold it in. Grief must be worked through to get to a place of healthy acceptance, remembrance and progressing with your life. It is natural to self-isolate oneself but when these tendencies become harmful- one must seek professional and guided mechanisms for healthy coping.

RELATIONSHIP PROBLEMS

Irrespective of how ideal a relationship is, be it family relationships, friendships or any other relationships, all relationships take work. Some relationships, however, are more difficult and require proportionately more work. We have to learn how to accommodate and adapt to their idiosyncrasies, their faults, their moods just as they must learn how to do the same with us. However, if a relationship gets too toxic- it is imperative to seek external help.

EATING DISORDER
AND BODY IMAGE

Body image is how we perceive ourselves when we look in the mirror. It can impact how we feel about ourselves and how others perceive us in relationships. It’s natural to find flaws in ourselves, but people with negative body image may take these negative feelings further, hating to look at their own bodies and obsessively pursuing an “ideal body”. The key to developing a positive body image is to recognize and respect our natural shape and learn to overpower those negative thoughts and feelings with acceptance that is positive and affirming. Seeking help from a clinical professional can go a long way in improving overcoming eating disorders and body-image issues.

PERSONALITY AND
IDENTITY CONCERNS

Everyone occasionally feels down, disappointed, or upset. These normal feelings can be triggered by events, perceptions, and expectations. But when such feelings turn into perpetual self-doubt and criticism, one gets lost in trying to define themself in a confident and distinct manner. Going through such confusing and paralyzing emotions can turn to inflicting harm on oneself or other, and to prevent such extremities one must seek professional counseling and guidance. Understanding ways to overcome such paralyzing tendencies highly benefit from clinical intervention.

SUBSTANCE ABUSE

Students may abuse alcohol or other drugs in an attempt to deal with the many stresses of college life as well as a way to deal with symptoms of anxiety and depression. Alcohol and other substances may initially seem to provide a means to “relax” but will eventually lead to many problems. Alcohol and substance abuse lead to interference in academic performance, relationship loss/changes, accidents as well as assaults. To prevent such misguided coping mechanisms and/or to get better if one is going through such a scenario, external mediation is necessary and highly recommended.

ACADEMIC CONCERNS

It is quite common for students to experience academic concerns at some point during their college career. While it is necessary to carry a healthy co-scholastic performa, it is very important to maintain a decent scholastic performance. This may pose as an overbearing concern for some, to find a way to balance everything along with an acceptable score card. When such panic arises, it is a precursor to degradation of mental wellness and stability. It is then advised to seek outside help and fix strategies to achieve desired balance.

FAMILY PROBLEMS
AND PATERNAL CONFLICT

Students may undergo difficulties not in the outside world, but rather in the inside world of their home. Parental conflict and family problems may be so immobilizing that a students becomes unable to function properly due to the stress. While coping with such stress is feasible, it is often not enough to deal with it alone. Such stress is eased greatly by intervention of a clinical professional. Understanding what causes this stress and finding ways to mitigate it becomes easier when there is another unbiased third-party listening to you.

SUICIDAL CONCERNS

Suicide is the second leading cause of death among college students, killing more young people between ages of 18-24 than all physical illnesses combined. Suicide is associated with feelings of helplessness, hopelessness and a need for escape. People who are suicidal are often quite ambivalent about ending their lives, and hence are willing to get counseling when a caring person facilitates the process for them. Understanding such concerns, and taking steps to revert to a functioning state-of-mind, is a time sensitive operation. It is therefore essential to take professional measures to help the students.
